About us

Initiative

Autism Teenage Partnership (ATP) is an initiative dedicated to providing accessible, free, and safe spaces for teens diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) in the Toronto (ATP-TO) and Kitchener-Waterloo (ATP-KW) regions. We support individuals with ASD by delivering weekly social programming that enables them to express themselves through games, crafts, and team-building activities.

ATP-TO currently runs every Wednesday nights from 6pm to 8pm at Milliken Park Community Recreation Centre, located at the south-east corner of McCowan Road and Steeles Avenue E.

Our newest chapter, ATP-KW is scheduled to launch on Saturday June 6th and will continue every Saturdays from 7pm to 9pm. Location is TBA.

History

The Autism Awareness initiative was started as a university club at Western University by Janson Chan and Brandon Wildfong. The club became known as Autism Awareness at Western (AAW) and continues to run today with over 120 members raising awareness of ASD.

When Janson returned to the GTA to study at YorkU, he became involved with the Agent's of Change Program allowing him to establish Autism Awareness at York (AAY). AAY was launched as Autism Teenage Partnership in the summer of 2014 and has helped over 25 participants in the local GTA and neighbouring Markham area.

Our program continues to grow with widespread success and continued support for teens with ASD and their families. More recently, we are happy to announce ATP's expansion into the Kitchener-Waterloo region spearheaded by Denise Dye.

Mission

Autism Teenage Partnership aims to positively impact the lives of individuals diagnosed with autism and their families through:

  1. Fostering the development of social skills and friendships to enhance quality of life and form social support networks.
  2. Allow for easy integration into society through the creation of inclusive and safe environments.
  3. Work with local agencies to develop relationships and raise awareness of Autism Spectrum Disorders.
  4. Develop toolkits for others to initiate ATP Chapters in underserved regions in Ontario.

    Autism Teenage Partnership is now in Kitchener-Waterloo! And the first teen get-together is Saturday, June 6th!

Outings and activities are chosen by participants, and every night is supported by trained university volunteers. This teen night helps to build confidence and independence, and provides members with a sense of belonging. Sponsors

  • Autism Ontario - Toronto & Waterloo

  • York University - Faculty of Health

    Agents of Change: http://stong.yorku.ca/agents-of-change/

    YorkU's Faculty of Health-Agents of Change Program has provided funding, engagement and media opportunities with a recent feature in Maclean's Magazine.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Where are you located?

    ATP-TO is held at Milliken Park Community Recreation Centre which is located at the south-east corner of McCowan Road and Steeles Avenue. Address: 4325 McCowan Rd, Scarborough, ON M1V 4P1

    ATP-KW location is TBA

  • When do you run your program?

    ATP-TO is held year round every Wednesdays nights from 6pm to 8pm.

    ATP-KW is scheduled to begin on Saturday June 6 and will be held year round every Saturday from 7pm to 9pm.

  • What are the current services you offer?

    Currently, ATP-TO offers weekly social programming for teens with ASD such as board games, crafts, and team-building activities co-ordinated by our program director. We are in the process of working towards providing field trips and excursions.

    ATP-KW provides similar activities with outdoor excursions planned every other week.

  • What ages do you serve?

    Our participants vary in age from 14 years to 25 years. However, ATP welcomes individuals of all ages.

  • Do I have to pay for your program?

    No. Our program is provided to individuals and their families free of charge!

  • Do you have any volunteering opportunities?

    Yes. We recruit university students who have a passion for working with teens on the autism spectrum. All volunteers will be trained and are required to have a police check prior to beginning their volunteer position with us. Police checks will be reimbursed.

    For further ATP-TO volunteer inquiries you may contact Brandon Wildfong at autismteenagepartnership@gmail.com

    For further ATP-KW volunteer inquiries you may contact Denise Dye at dye@whereisit.com

  • How are you funded?

    Autism Teenage Partnership is largely funded by Milliken Park Advisory Council, the Registered Nurses Association of Ontario-Region 7, York University, and Autism Ontario.
